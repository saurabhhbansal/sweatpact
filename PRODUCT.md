# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Competitive fitness pairs — friends, partners, or gym duos who already train and want social accountability with financial stakes. Their context: they're competing, not just tracking. The primary job is showing up and making the other person pay when they don't. Every session is a data point in a live contest.

## Product Purpose

SweatPact is a gym accountability platform that pairs two people in a challenge with real money on the line. Users commit to a weekly gym goal, verify check-ins via location, and settle up based on who showed up. The cycle tracking layer adds a second competitive vector for female users. Success looks like: a user who would have skipped leg day doesn't, because they'd owe their partner.

## Brand Personality

Competitive · Charged · Sharp. The energy of a Strava leaderboard combined with the seriousness of Whoop data and the social tension of a Snapchat streak. No fluff. Every number shown has consequence.

## Anti-references

- **MyFitnessPal / generic fitness trackers**: Clinical, chart-heavy, feels like homework. SweatPact is not a logging tool.
- **Standard SaaS dashboards**: Gray sidebar nav, metric cards in grids, corporate productivity feel. Zero personality.
- **Nike Run Club / Under Armour**: Brand-forward, logo-heavy, corporate athlete aesthetic. More marketing than product.

## Design Principles

1. **Stakes, not stats** — Every number has a consequence: streak count, money owed, check-ins missed. Surface only what changes the outcome.
2. **Social pressure is the feature** — The head-to-head competitive loop between two people is the core mechanic. Design amplifies that tension, not personal bests.
3. **Show, don't explain** — The score should be legible without reading. A verified check-in, a missed day, a winning streak — each must feel categorically different at a glance.
4. **Own the streak** — Every interaction either protects or threatens the streak. Design with that as the primary emotional lever.
5. **One platform, full identity** — SweatPact is not a fitness tracker, not a social app, not a payment app. It's all three, distilled. Remove anything that blurs that specificity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Don't rely on color alone to convey check-in status — support icon + color. Reduced motion: all animations must have `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives. Cycle tracking features should be gender-neutral in copy (currently scoped to female users by profile; keep that gating non-presumptuous).
